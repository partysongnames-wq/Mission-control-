Kobe

Famous for Kobe beef, night views from Mt. Rokko, and the charming Kitano-cho foreign residences. Easy stop on the Tokaido/Sanyo line.

Akashi

Home of the world's longest suspension bridge (Akashi Kaikyo) and Akashi-yaki — a softer, eggier take on takoyaki. Octopus is the local obsession.

Mojiko

Retro port town in Kitakyushu. Yaki curry, historic Mojiko Station, and Kanmon Strait views. Near Kokura but a distinct vibe.

Hagi

Samurai castle town on the Sea of Japan. Preserved Edo-era streets, pottery, and a slower pace. North coast — longer trip.

Kinosaki Onsen

Classic hot spring town with seven public baths and wooden ryokans. Stroll in yukata between baths. Near Himeji via regional train.

Tsuyama

Castle town with one of Japan's best original keeps. Less touristed, good for a quiet wander. North of Okayama.

Saijo (Higashi-Hiroshima)

One of Japan's top sake brewery districts. Walking tour through historic kura (storehouses) with tastings. Easy from Hiroshima.

Yamaguchi City

From Shin-Yamaguchi, the Ruriko-ji five-story pagoda and peaceful temple grounds. The "Kyoto of the West" without crowds.

Worth considering?

If Shimonoseki's Kanmon Strait appeal felt underrated in the rankings, Mojiko across the strait pairs well with it. Same for Saijo if you want sake culture without the Kyoto tourist crush.
