## Teaching Waiver Summary

- **3 waivers max** (2 years each = 6 years total)
- After third waiver expires: must complete TPDI 7-module training (420 hours) for permanent licence
- **Escape hatch**: Completing 4 of 7 modules upgrades you to P-License, bypassing the waiver cap
- Switching schools mid-contract burns a waiver (new school applies for fresh one)

## Marriage Visa Option (Non-O)

### Benefits
- Stay in Thailand based on marriage, not employment
- Not tied to any single employer
- Makes you more attractive to employers (they only do work permit, not visa)
- Can do side work more freely (still technically needs permit, but lower risk)

### Conversion Process (Non-B to Non-O)
1. Current visa needs 15+ days remaining
2. Have 400k THB in Thai bank for 2-3 months OR proof of 40k/month income
3. Gather documents
4. Apply at Immigration → get 90-day stamp
5. Before expiry, apply for 1-year extension
6. Renew yearly

### Income Proof (40k/month option)
Since Australia no longer issues income affidavits, options are:
- **Thai salary**: Work permit + pay slips + tax docs (PND.1, PND91) + social security
- **International transfers**: 12 months of 40k+ transfers from abroad into Thai bank (in your name)

### Documents Needed

**From Trent:**
- Passport (6+ months validity, 2 blank pages)
- Bank book OR income proof
- Photos together (house, family pics)
- Map to residence

**From wife:**
- Thai ID card
- House registration (tabien baan)
- Marriage certificate (Thai version)

### Important Notes
- Immigration may do home visit to verify relationship
- Need re-entry permit before leaving Thailand (1,000 THB single / 3,800 THB multiple)
- 90-day reporting still applies
- Divorce = visa cancelled immediately

## Employer Benefits (Hiring Foreigner with Thai Spouse)
- Only 2 Thai employees required (instead of 4)
- Only 1 million THB registered capital (instead of 2 million)
